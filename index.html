My political ambitions

Future UK Politician

Aspiring Member of Parliament · UK Politics
I Want to Change Things

I am a young adult from England with a plan: enter UK politics and one day become a Member of Parliament. Not to play the game, but to change it. I want to be the kind of politician this country actually needs: young, queer, working class, honest, and fighting for everyone who gets left behind. Trans rights, digital rights, equality, housing, mental health. All of it. If nobody else is going to say it, I will :3 I stand firmly against extremism, fascism, Marxist-Leninism, and Stalinism. Economically, I am at my core a capitalist, but with an open mind. We need to learn from socialism to build a fairer system, just as socialism must learn from capitalism.

🏛️
Heading to Westminster

I want a seat at the table in the UK Parliament where decisions get made. Every part of the UK, from Scotland to the South of England, deserves strong, honest representation.

🏳️‍⚧️
Trans Rights in Law

I would fight to enshrine trans rights into UK law: full legal recognition, access to healthcare, and protection from discrimination. No compromises.

💻
Digital Rights

Tech should serve people, not corporations. I would push for open-source government systems, data privacy legislation, and fair AI regulation.

🌍
Equality for All

Race, gender, sexuality, disability, class. Every axis of inequality matters. I believe in intersectional politics that lifts everyone up together.

🏠
Housing and Poverty

Young people and working class communities are being priced out of life. Affordable housing, a real living wage, and an end to food bank culture are non-negotiables.

🧠
Mental Health

The NHS mental health system is broken. CAMHS waiting lists are a genuine scandal, and too many people do not get support until it is too late.

My stance
TRANS
RIGHTS
01
Identity is Real

Trans people have existed across every culture and throughout all of recorded history. Gender identity is a documented part of human experience. The science is clear and the lived experiences are undeniable.

02
Healthcare Saves Lives

Gender-affirming care is evidence-based medicine backed by every major medical organisation worldwide. Access to it reduces depression, anxiety, and suicidality. Denying it causes real, measurable harm.

03
Safety Matters

Trans people face disproportionate rates of violence and discrimination. Hate crimes are rising. Protecting trans rights is a matter of basic human safety, not ideology.

04
Sport is for Everyone

The moral panic around trans athletes is not backed by evidence of systematic advantage. Trans women are women. Those who claim to oppose only trans women in women's sport consistently support banning trans people from all sport, including ungendered competitions. That is not a sports policy. That is exclusion.

05
Youth Deserve Support

Anyone experiencing gender dysphoria, especially from around age 13, should receive proper mental health support in a medically safe and well-regulated environment. Early, careful support improves outcomes. Conversion therapy causes documented trauma, full stop.

06
Simply Put

Trans rights are human rights. Trans people are not a debate, not a culture war, and not a threat. We are people living our lives. Respect costs nothing and means everything :3 🏳️‍⚧️
